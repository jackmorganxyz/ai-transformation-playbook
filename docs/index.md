# AI Augmentation Playbook

## 1. What is this playbook?

A step-by-step manual for augmenting your workforce with AI.

The goal is not to replace employees. It is to bolt an AI co-worker onto every
role so each person produces two to three times more output for a fraction of
the cost of hiring another head.

This playbook walks you through analysing your company, identifying the
workflows that actually drive value, and systematically automating everything
else so your people can focus on the work that matters.

## 2. Why use this playbook?

A $100,000 employee augmented with a $10,000 AI that makes them two to three
times more productive is the highest-leverage investment a company can make
right now.

Organizations doing this math are permanently outpacing competitors. The ones
that hesitate are trying to match that output with raw headcount, and they are
running out of runway.

This is not about cutting people. Companies that successfully remove execution
bottlenecks through AI augmentation grow faster and hire _more_ people to
direct the expanding output. But the job description changes: you stop hiring
manual executors and start hiring AI-augmented operators.

The playbook exists because most companies know they should be doing this but
have no structured way to figure out _where_ to start, _what_ to automate, and
_how_ to do it safely.

## 3. The steps (plain version)

Here is what you will do, in order:

1. **Analyse the company.** Understand the business model, how money is made,
   what systems are in use, and where the constraints are.

2. **Map each role's workflows.** For every employee or role, identify the
   specific tasks they do day to day. Separate the high-value work (judgment,
   relationships, decisions) from the low-value work (status updates, data
   entry, copy-paste, formatting, chasing people).

3. **Score each task for automation fit.** Use a simple rubric to rate every
   task on business impact, frequency, how standardized the process is, data
   readiness, reversibility, compliance sensitivity, and how much human
   judgment it needs.

4. **Pick what to automate first.** Choose one workflow that scores high on
   automation fit, is low risk, and will produce a visible win. This is your
   pilot.

5. **Design the AI augmentation.** Specify exactly what the AI agent does, what
   it does not do, where a human still approves, what data it touches, and how
   you roll it back if something goes wrong.

6. **Check security, deployment, and cost.** Before anything goes live, review
   data access, compliance, whether to run on-prem or hosted, and what the
   token costs will look like.

7. **Launch, measure, and expand.** Start in shadow mode (AI runs but a human
   still does the work), graduate to draft-review mode (AI does the work, human
   approves), then expand. Track whether it is actually saving time and
   improving quality.

The outcome: every employee has an AI co-worker handling the repetitive
execution work, freeing them to focus on the judgment, relationships, and
creative work that no model can replace.

## Ground rules

- Start with a workflow, not a model.
- Augment the employee, do not replace the employee.
- Keep approval points explicit.
- Treat security, deployment, and rollback as design inputs.
- Keep the core framework vendor-neutral.

## Problem Statement

Account managers need a concise view of delivery, sentiment, and next steps, but
that view usually has to be assembled manually before each client interaction.

## Trigger

Run when an account review or client meeting prep cycle begins.

## Inputs

- CRM stage and activity notes
- delivery summary and open issues
- last meeting actions and owners

## Systems/Tools

- CRM
- delivery tracker
- notes workspace

## Workflow Steps

1. Pull the latest account, delivery, and meeting artifacts.
2. Cluster open issues, decisions, and follow-ups by theme.
3. Draft an account health brief with explicit uncertainty markers.
4. Suggest watch-outs and owner next steps for review.

## Human Approval Points

- Account owner edits all client-facing recommendations.
- Leadership-facing risk statements are confirmed before publication.

## Outputs

- account prep brief
- action list by owner
- relationship and delivery watch-outs

## Success Metrics

- lower prep time
- better follow-up completion
- fewer missed relationship risks

## Failure Modes

- a subtle client signal is missed
- a pipeline risk is overstated
- duplicated notes create noisy recommendations

## Security Notes

- keep account data within approved systems
- avoid auto-posting summaries to shared client channels

## Token-Cost Notes

- focus on the latest cycle and unresolved risks
- compress repeated meeting notes into structured action records

## Deployment Mode

`Hybrid` is appropriate because the agent prepares material efficiently, but the
relationship owner remains accountable for interpretation and outreach.

## Prompt Skeleton

```text
Role: Account prep analyst

Objective: Create a concise internal account health brief for review.

Inputs: CRM notes, delivery summary, open actions, recent meeting notes.

Constraints: Distinguish confirmed facts from inferred risk. Never draft direct client promises as final.

Required checks: Highlight anything the account owner must verify before external use.

Output format: Account summary, risks, opportunities, open actions, watch-outs.
```

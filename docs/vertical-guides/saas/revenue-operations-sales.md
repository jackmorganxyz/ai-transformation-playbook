# SaaS: Revenue Operations / Sales

## Industry Context

Revenue teams lose time on CRM hygiene, post-meeting updates, and next-step
tracking. Those tasks are structured enough to support automation, but deal
strategy still requires human judgment.

## Common Low-Value Task Clusters

- summarizing sales calls into CRM updates
- flagging missing next steps or stale opportunities
- drafting follow-up tasks and owner reminders
- normalizing notes into stage-specific fields

## Recommended Agent Opportunities

- pipeline hygiene review
- next-step draft generation
- opportunity risk flagging from call notes

## Constraints & Compliance Notes

- forecast decisions remain human-owned
- customer commitments should not be auto-generated or auto-sent
- CRM write-backs need auditability and rollback

## Role Map

| Adjacent role | Hand-off |
| --- | --- |
| Sales rep | Reviews and applies updates |
| RevOps manager | Owns taxonomy and reporting quality |
| Sales leader | Uses outputs for forecast review |

## Scorecard Usage

Post-call normalization and hygiene tasks often land in `partial automation`.
Forecasting, pricing judgment, and deal negotiation usually stay human-led or
agent-assisted.

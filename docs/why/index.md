# Why AI Augmentation

## The math

Take a $100,000 employee. Give them a $10,000 AI that makes them two to three
times more productive. You now get $200,000 to $300,000 of output for $110,000.

Do that across every role and the math becomes overwhelming. Organizations
executing this equation permanently outpace competitors who try to match output
by hiring more people.

## Augmentation, not replacement

The counterintuitive result: companies that remove execution bottlenecks through
AI grow faster and end up hiring _more_ people, not fewer. Output expands so
much that you need more humans to direct the expanding work.

What changes is the job description. You stop hiring people to do manual
execution and start hiring people who can operate with AI — people who provide
direction while the machine provides velocity.

## High-value work vs. low-value execution

Every employee's work breaks down into two categories:

1. **High-value work** — judgment, relationships, creative problem-solving,
   decisions that require context and experience.
2. **Low-value execution** — status updates, data entry, formatting,
   copy-paste, chasing follow-ups, assembling reports from existing data.

Most employees spend the majority of their time on category two.

This playbook gives you a repeatable method to identify which tasks are which,
score them for automation fit, design AI agents that handle the execution, and
keep humans in control of the judgment.

The goal: shift every employee's time from execution to judgment. The AI handles
the bandwidth. The human handles the direction.

## What this playbook optimizes for

- Fast comprehension for first-time readers
- Reusable documents instead of workshop-only artifacts
- Vendor-neutral guidance in the core framework
- Explicit review boundaries before anything touches live work
- Augmentation over replacement at every step

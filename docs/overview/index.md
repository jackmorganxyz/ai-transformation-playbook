# Why AI Augmentation

## The core idea

Take a $100,000 employee. Give them a $10,000 AI. That AI makes them two to
three times more productive. You now get $200,000 to $300,000 worth of output
for $110,000.

Do that across every role in the company and the math becomes overwhelming.
Organizations executing this equation are permanently outpacing competitors
who are still trying to match output by hiring more people.

## This is not about replacing people

The counterintuitive result: companies that successfully remove execution
bottlenecks through AI augmentation grow faster and end up hiring _more_
people, not fewer. Output expands so much that you need more humans to direct
the expanding work.

What changes is the job description. You stop hiring people to do manual
execution. You start hiring people who can operate with AI, people who provide
direction while the machine provides velocity.

## What the playbook actually does

Every employee's work breaks down into two categories:

1. **High-value work** — judgment, relationships, creative problem-solving,
   decisions that require context and experience.
2. **Low-value execution** — status updates, data entry, formatting,
   copy-paste, chasing follow-ups, assembling reports from existing data.

Most employees spend the majority of their time on category two.

This playbook gives you a repeatable method to:

- Identify exactly which tasks in each role are high-value and which are
  low-value execution
- Score each task for how well it fits AI automation
- Design AI agents that handle the execution work
- Keep humans in control of the judgment work
- Roll out safely with clear approval points and rollback plans

The goal is to shift every employee's time from execution to judgment. The AI
handles the bandwidth. The human handles the direction.

## How the playbook is structured

The playbook follows a linear sequence. Each step produces a document you use
in the next step.

| Step | What you do | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Analyse the company](../company-research/index.md) | Research business model, workflows, systems, constraints | You cannot augment what you do not understand |
| [Map workflows](../opportunity-mapping/index.md) | Break each role into specific tasks | Augmentation targets must be concrete, not vague |
| [Score tasks](../task-scoring/index.md) | Rate each task on automation fit vs. human value | Prevents automating the wrong things |
| [Pick the pilot](../automation-design/index.md) | Choose the best first candidate | A visible early win builds momentum |
| [Design the augmentation](../automation-design/agent-design-brief.md) | Specify the AI agent's scope, approvals, and boundaries | Keeps humans in control |
| [Review constraints](../security/index.md) | Check security, deployment, and cost | Prevents launches that violate policy or blow budgets |
| [Launch and govern](../automation-design/pilot-rollout-measurement-governance.md) | Staged rollout with monitoring | Catches problems before they scale |

## What this playbook optimizes for

- Fast comprehension for first-time readers
- Reusable documents instead of workshop-only artifacts
- Vendor-neutral guidance in the core framework
- Explicit review boundaries before anything touches live work
- Augmentation over replacement at every step

# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This project exists to publish practical, vendor-neutral guidance. Productive
contribution depends on clear disagreement, evidence-backed claims, and respect
for the people doing the work.

## Expected behavior

- Be direct, specific, and professional.
- Anchor claims in real workflows, data, or source material.
- Distinguish clearly between observed facts, assumptions, and opinions.
- Flag security, compliance, and human-impact concerns early.
- Leave the repo better structured than you found it.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, harassment, or intimidation.
- Marketing copy presented as neutral guidance.
- Dismissing security, compliance, or human review concerns without argument.
- Publishing proprietary, personal, or sensitive customer material.
- Repeated low-effort submissions that ignore the documented templates.

## Moderation

Maintainers may edit, hide, or reject contributions that do not meet the
project standard. Repeated violations may lead to loss of contribution
privileges.

## Reporting

Open a private maintainer contact through GitHub if a conduct issue cannot be
handled in public. Include links, dates, and the smallest amount of context
needed to review the issue.
